5.02 Introduction to the Constitution

#1 Shay’s Rebellion was an uprising in Massachusetts, took place from August 1786 until February 1787. It was a response to economic problems the new nation faced. Many soldiers could not afford to pay taxes. During this revolt, protesters blocked the courts so people could not be punished for not paying taxes.  

#2 Drafters of the Constitution

  • Alexander Hamilton was a strong supporter of the idea of creating a new and improved constitution for the United States.
  • James Wilson wrote the first draft of the United States Constitution.
  • James Madison recorded all of the information that was talked about at the Constitutional Convention.
  • Benjamin Franklin was a key person involved in the drafting of the United States Constitution.

The Great Compromise

Each state would have two representatives in the senate, and their representation in the House of Representatives would be based on population. This was the compromise. Parts of each proposal were included in the new plan.

Political concepts of the Constitution

  • Liberty - the ability to do what one wishes without being prevented from doing so
  • Individual rights
  • Democracy

  • Representative government -a type of government that is run by people who have been elected

  • Limited Government - a type of government that has powers that are restricted.

An important philosopher

Aristotle

Aristotle was one of the first to discuss government by the people. He believed that a constitution could organize the way of life for a community and ensure that citizens have freedom. He believed in limited power for leaders.

Thomas Hobbes

Hobbes promoted the idea of liberty in his work. He showed that individuals actually have more liberty under the rule of government. He thought that life without government would be dangerous.

John Locke

Rights were a very central part of Locke's philosophy. He believed that everyone has natural rights, which are privileges or claims that people are entitled to. In particular, he believed that man has the right to life, liberty, and property.
